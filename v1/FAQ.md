# FAQ: ALEM License

Frequently Asked Questions about the ALEM License.

### 1. What does "ALEM" stand for?
ALEM is the Kazakh word for "The World" or "Universe" (Әлем). As an acronym for this license, it stands for:
* **A**ttribution — You must credit the author.
* **L**earning — Free for education and AI training.
* **E**quity — Fair rules for commercial usage.
* **M**odification — Specific rules for authorship (50% threshold).

### 2. Can I use ALEM-licensed code for training my AI model?
**Yes.** The ALEM license explicitly allows the use of content for training Artificial Intelligence models without any additional permissions or fees.

### 3. I want to use this project in my paid app. What should I do?
You are free to do so, but you **must** provide a visible attribution. This means you should mention the original author's name and provide a link to the original repository in your app's "About" section, documentation, or credits.

### 4. How is the "50% modification rule" measured?
It is measured by the number of **newly added lines** of functional content or code. If your version consists of 100 lines and 60 of them are original (from the ALEM repo) and only 40 are yours, you cannot claim yourself as the primary author. You must keep the original copyright notice intact.

### 5. Can I remove the original author's name if I rewrite most of the code?
**Yes.** Once your modifications exceed **50% of the total volume** (based on added lines), you are considered to have transformed the work significantly enough to claim primary authorship of your version, though mentioning the "base" project is still encouraged as a matter of professional courtesy.
